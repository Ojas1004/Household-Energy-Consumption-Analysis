Dataset Overview

This analysis utilizes the Appliance Energy Prediction dataset from the UCI Machine Learning Repository. The dataset comprises 19,735 observations collected over two periods in 2010 at a house in Skovlunde, Denmark: January 11 – February 21 and June 5 – 19. Measurements were taken at 10-minute intervals, capturing appliance energy consumption (Wh), lighting energy consumption (Wh), indoor and outdoor temperature (°C), relative humidity (%), wind speed (m/s), and atmospheric pressure (mm Hg). This comprehensive dataset provides the foundation for understanding household energy consumption patterns and their relationships with environmental factors, making it ideal for statistical analysis and predictive modeling.

Analysis Methodology

Our analysis employs multiple statistical techniques to extract meaningful insights from the data. Descriptive statistics (mean, median, variance, standard deviation) characterize the central tendency and variability of energy consumption variables. Correlation analysis examines relationships between appliance energy use and other variables. Hypothesis testing evaluates whether lighting levels significantly affect appliance energy consumption. Probability analysis estimates the likelihood of specific energy consumption thresholds, while Monte Carlo simulations model potential future values under varying conditions. These methods are integrated to provide a holistic understanding of household energy consumption dynamics.
