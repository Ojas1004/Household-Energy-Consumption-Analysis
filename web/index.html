Household Energy Consumption Analysis

Comprehensive statistical analysis of appliance energy consumption patterns using the Appliance Energy Prediction dataset. This analysis employs descriptive statistics, correlation analysis, probability theory, hypothesis testing, and Monte Carlo simulations to understand household energy usage patterns and the relationship between appliance and lighting energy consumption.

UCI Dataset Context

Location: Skovlunde, Denmark

Time Period: January 11 – February 21 and June 5 – 19, 2016

Observations: 19,735 records at 10-minute intervals

Variables: 29 columns including energy consumption, environmental, and weather data

Analysis Methodology

Descriptive Statistics: Mean, median, mode, variance, standard deviation

Relationship Analysis: Pearson correlation and scatter visualization

Probability Estimation: Empirical probability for specified thresholds

Hypothesis Testing: Comparison of means between groups

Simulation: Monte Carlo method for forecasting possible values
